About the Provincial Rice Technology Forum (PRTF)

Davao del Sur | Dry Season 2026 (October 2025 ‚Äì March 2026)

The Provincial Rice Technology Forum (PRTF) ‚Äì Davao del Sur is a collaborative initiative led by the Office of the Provincial Agriculturist (OPAG) in partnership with DA-RFO XI, NIA, PhilRice, ATI, Davao del Sur State College (DSSC), LGUs, and private agri-input companies. Modeled after the National Rice Techno Forum (NRTF), the PRTF is a season-long field demonstration designed to showcase high-yielding rice technologies, sustainable farming practices, and innovative fertilizer systems.

  • Introduce and demonstrate high-yielding hybrid rice varieties and sustainable practices.
  • Promote adoption of modern, climate-resilient rice farming methods.
  • Facilitate knowledge exchange among farmers, researchers, and private sector partners.
  • Foster public-private collaboration to accelerate agricultural development.

üë• Who Can Participate?

  • Private Companies (PPCs): Seed, fertilizer, and fertilizer-related product providers.
  • Farmer Cooperators (FCs): Registered farmers with suitable land and willingness to follow protocols.

üìã Participation Process

  1. Site Identification: MLGU (Matanao MAO) identifies demo sites; validated by DA-PLGU-NIA for irrigation and accessibility.
  2. Pre-registration: PPCs submit PRTF Form 1 (Intent to Join) by August 13, 2025.
  3. Protocol Finalization: PLGU presents and finalizes the PRTF Protocol with all stakeholders.
  4. Official Registration: PPCs submit PRTF Form 2 with workplan, passport data (for seeds), and ‚Ç±5,000 registration fee per entry.
  5. FC-PPC Pairing: Qualified FCs are matched with PPCs for joint implementation.

üöú Field Demonstration Mechanics

  • Plot Size: Minimum 0.5 hectare per entry (max 2 entries/company).
  • GPS Measurement: Geo-referenced plots ensure fairness.
  • Transplanting: 50-50 cost sharing between PPC and FC.
  • Input Disclosure: All inputs and costs must be transparent and verifiable.
  • Signage: Uniform placards (3.5 x 2 ft) designed by PLGU, printed by PPC.
  • Yield: 100% of harvest goes to the Farmer Cooperator.

üìä Monitoring & Evaluation

The Composite Team (CT) ‚Äî composed of OPAG, MAO, FCs, DA-RFO XI, and DSSC ‚Äî oversees:

  • Documentation of all field activities.
  • Soil and microbial analysis (before and after for bio-fertilizers).
  • Data collection at critical stages (tillering, flowering, harvest).
  • Crop-cutting in 10 sampling areas (2m x 2.5m) per plot.
  • Validation of yield and ROI analysis by DA-Research and DSSC.

  • Sabotage, bribery, or misrepresentation.
  • Abandoning the entry for 2 weeks without communication.
  • Non-compliance with the PRTF Protocol.
