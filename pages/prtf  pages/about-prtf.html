About the Provincial Rice Technology Forum (PRTF)

Davao del Sur | Dry Season 2026 (October 2025 ‚Äì March 2026)

The Provincial Rice Technology Forum (PRTF) ‚Äì Davao del Sur is a collaborative initiative led by the Office of the Provincial Agriculturist (OPAG) in partnership with DA-RFO XI, NIA, PhilRice, ATI, Davao del Sur State College (DSSC), LGUs, and private agri-input companies. Modeled after the National Rice Techno Forum (NRTF), the PRTF is a season-long field demonstration designed to showcase high-yielding rice technologies, sustainable farming practices, and innovative fertilizer systems.

üéØ Objectives

  • Introduce and demonstrate high-yielding hybrid rice varieties and sustainable practices.
  • Promote adoption of modern, climate-resilient rice farming methods.
  • Facilitate knowledge exchange among farmers, researchers, and private sector partners.
  • Foster public-private collaboration to accelerate agricultural development.

üë• Key Players & Their Roles

The success of the PRTF DS2026 relies on the active participation and collaboration of multiple stakeholders. Below are the detailed roles and responsibilities of each key player:

PLGU/OPAG (Lead-Convenor)

  • Serve as the primary organizer and overall coordinator of the PRTF.
  • Prepare the PRTF protocol, guidelines, and all necessary forms.
  • Provide fertilizer support: 1 bag urea + 1 bag complete per 0.5 hectare.
  • Organize and manage major events (Ceremonial Planting, Harvest Festival).
  • Design the official plot signage template for all participants.
  • Coordinate with DA-RFO XI, MLGUs, and other partner agencies.
  • Ensure proper documentation and data collection throughout the season.
  • Facilitate the drawing of lots for plot assignment.

MLGU/MAO (Assistant Lead-Convenor)

  • Identify feasible techno-demonstration sites within the municipality.
  • Lead the identification and profiling of prospective Farmer Cooperators (FCs).
  • Prepare detailed vicinity maps of the demonstration area.
  • Conduct geo-tagging and geo-referencing of all demonstration plots.
  • Coordinate with Irrigator Associations (IAs) and farmer groups.
  • Provide logistical support for field activities and events.
  • Participate in the staking of sampling areas and monitoring activities.
  • Select the hybrid rice variety for the Balanced Fertilization Technique (BFT) Bout.

DA-RFO XI (Overall Supervisor)

  • Provide overall supervision and technical oversight of the PRTF.
  • Supply the hybrid seeds for the Balanced Fertilization Technique (BFT) Bout.
  • Coordinate with NIA for timely release of irrigation water.
  • Provide technical guidance and extension support to all participants.
  • Facilitate the participation of research and development agencies (PhilRice, ATI).
  • Support in the validation of data and performance evaluation.
  • Ensure alignment with national rice development programs and policies.
  • Monitor compliance with the PRTF protocol.

Private Companies (PPCs)

  • Pay a registration fee of ‚Ç±5,000 per entry.
  • Submit a detailed workplan and passport data for seed varieties.
  • Provide all inputs (fertilizers, pesticides, growth regulators) for their entries.
  • Assign technical personnel to supervise field activities.
  • Print and install the official plot signage at their demonstration plots.
  • Provide T-shirts and arm sleeves for their partnered Farmer Cooperators.
  • Share 50% of the transplanting costs with the FC.
  • Disclose all inputs and operations to the monitoring team.

Farmer Cooperators (FCs)

  • Provide land with a minimum area of 0.5 hectare for the demonstration plot.
  • Share 50% of the transplanting costs with the participating private company.
  • Provide labor for land preparation, weeding, and other crop care activities.
  • Pay irrigation fees and enroll in PCIC for crop insurance.
  • Allow monitoring and documentation by the Composite Team.
  • Retain 100% of the harvest from their demonstration plot.
  • Submit actual yield data for evaluation and validation.
  • Participate in all PRTF-related activities and events.

DSSC & DA-Research (Evaluators)

  • Conduct performance evaluation of all PRTF entries.
  • Perform soil sampling and microbial analysis (for bio-fertilizer entries).
  • Collect data at critical growth stages (tillering, flowering, harvest).
  • Conduct crop-cutting in 10 sampling areas per plot (2m x 2.5m).
  • Validate yield and return on investment (ROI) analysis.
  • Determine the top 5 highest-performing entries based on yield and cost-efficiency.
  • Ensure scientific rigor and transparency in the evaluation process.
  • Submit official results to the lead convenor for recognition.

üìã How to Participate

Private companies and farmer cooperators who wish to join the PRTF DS2026 can follow this step-by-step registration process:

1. Fill Out the Pre-Registration Form (PRTF Form 1)

Submit your intent to join by completing the Pre-Registration Form (PRTF Form 1) . This step formally expresses your interest in participating in the PRTF Davao del Sur program for the upcoming dry season.

2. Read Guidelines & Receive Confirmation

After which if you decided to proced follow Step 3.

Note: Before proceeding, please read and fully understand the PRTF Davao del Sur Dry Season 2025‚Äì2026 Guidelines and Protocol sent via email or available on the official portal.

3. Fill out the Final Registration Form (PRTF Form 2) for the finality of your Product Entry

Proceed to fill out the Final Registration Form (PRTF Form 2) to finalize your product entry. Your registered email address will recieved a response of your registration together of your initial acknowledgement reciept and a link of a shared spreadsheet for your Package of Technology(POT) / Workplan (PRTF Form 3).

4. Accomplish Your POT / Work Plan (PRTF Form 3)

Using the shared spreadsheet link provided in your email, fill out the details needed to complete your Package of Technology (POT) / Work Plan (PRTF Form 3) with the following details per demo area:

  • Field operations and timeline
  • Cultural practices
  • Fertilization management
  • Pest and disease control strategies
  • Other relevant agricultural activities
üìù Note: Since the PRTF commences in the upcoming Dry Season 2025‚Äì2026, you may input tentative details initially. The spreadsheet is editable, allowing updates once final timelines and activities are confirmed.
5. Print, Sign, and Submit Hard Copies

Once all forms are completed, print, sign, and submit the following documents to the OPAG Davao del Sur-RICE Team:

  • Pre-Registration Form (PRTF Form 1)
  • Final Registration Form (PRTF Form 2) and Acknowledgment Receipt
  • Completed POT / Work Plan (PRTF Form 3)

Along with these documents, pay the required registration fee / product entry fee to the designated PRTF cashier.

üåæ Trial Categories

  • Hybrid Seed Varietal Trials:
    • Max 2 varieties per company.
    • No duplicate varieties allowed.
    • Must submit passport data for maturity-based sowing scheduling.
    • Late-maturing varieties established first based on maturity dates.
  • Balanced Fertilization Technique (BFT) Bout:
    • All entries use the same hybrid variety (provided by DA-RFO XI, selected by MLGU-MAO).
    • Focus on bio-fertilizers, soil ameliorants, and organic inputs as ‚Äúadd-on‚Äù to 80% of standard NPK.
    • Each plot split into ‚Äú+‚Äù (add-on) and ‚Äú‚Äì‚Äù (control) treatments (80:20 scheme).
    • Product entries must be commercially available and verifiable.

üöú Field Demonstration Mechanics

  • Plot Size: Minimum 0.5 hectare per entry (max 2 entries/company); measured by GPS/geo-referencing.
  • Land Preparation: Cost borne by FC; any additional prescribed activities (e.g., markers) paid by PPC.
  • Transplanting: 50-50 cost sharing between PPC and FC; ceremonial transplanting marks PRTF launching.
  • Input Disclosure: All inputs and costs must be transparent and verifiable; only commercially available technologies allowed.
  • Spacing: 20cm x 20cm for wet season; 20cm x 15cm for dry season to ensure desired plant population.
  • Signage: Uniform placards (3.5 x 2 ft with 6ft standee) designed by PLGU, printed and installed by PPC.
  • Yield: 100% of harvest goes to the Farmer Cooperator; FC must submit actual yield data.
  • Soil Analysis: Conducted before PRTF begins; bio-fertilizer entries require microbial analysis before and after.

üìä Monitoring & Evaluation

The Composite Team (CT) ‚Äî composed of OPAG, MAO, FCs, DA-RFO XI, and DSSC ‚Äî oversees all monitoring and evaluation activities:

  • Documentation of all field activities witnessed by CT members.
  • Soil and microbial analysis (before and after for bio-fertilizers).
  • Data collection at critical stages (tillering, flowering, harvest).
  • Crop-cutting in 10 sampling areas (2m x 2.5m) per plot.
  • Validation of yield and ROI analysis by DA-Research and DSSC.
  • Final results considered official and submitted to the lead convenor.

üö´ Grounds for Disqualification

  • Failure to disclose inputs or operations to the monitoring team.
  • Implementing field activities in the absence of the monitoring/documentation team.
  • Sabotage of other competing entries.
  • Bribery (directly or indirectly).
  • Abandoning the entry for 2 weeks without communication.
  • Non-compliance with the PRTF Protocol.
  • Misrepresentation or fraudulent publication of results.

Note: Disqualified entities will be barred from joining any government procurement through bidding.

üèÜ Recognition & Awards

The top 5 highest-performing entries ‚Äî based on yield (ton/ha at 14% moisture content) and cost-efficiency (low production cost) ‚Äî will be officially recognized during the Harvest Festival / Farmers' Field Day in February 2026.

The DA-Research team and Davao del Sur State College (DSSC) will validate all data and determine the winning entries, ensuring transparency and scientific rigor in the selection process.
